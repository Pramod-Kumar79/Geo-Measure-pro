Professional FAQ

What professional measurement standards does this calculator support?

Our calculator adheres to international surveying standards including:

  • ISO 19125 (Geographic information)
  • FGDC Cadastral Data Content Standard
  • Indian Survey Standards (Survey of India)
  • Nepal Survey Department standards

We support both traditional local units and international metric standards.

How accurate are the calculations for professional surveying work?

Our calculator provides professional-grade accuracy suitable for:

  • Precision to 6 decimal places for all calculations
  • Support for geodetic coordinate transformations
  • Ellipsoidal earth model corrections
  • Customizable rounding for legal descriptions

For high-precision cadastral surveys, we recommend using our professional desktop software with GNSS integration.

Can I import data from professional surveying equipment?

Yes, our professional version supports direct import from:

  • Total stations (Leica, Trimble, Topcon formats)
  • GNSS/GPS survey equipment
  • CAD files (DWG, DXF)
  • GIS formats (Shapefile, GeoJSON, KML)

Data can be exported to industry-standard formats for use in CAD and GIS applications.

How do I calculate area for irregular shaped properties?

For professional measurement of irregular parcels:

  1. Use our coordinate-based area calculator
  2. Input boundary points from your survey
  3. Apply the DMD (Double Meridian Distance) method
  4. Use the Simpson's Rule for curved boundaries
  5. Account for slope corrections if needed

Our software automatically applies professional algorithms for maximum accuracy.

What professional reports can I generate?

Our professional version generates survey-grade reports including:

  • Legal area certificates
  • Survey plats and sketches
  • Boundary descriptions
  • Calculated area summaries
  • Coordinate lists
  • Custom-branded reports

All reports comply with local land record requirements.

Professional Tips

Measurement Best Practices

For professional accuracy:

  1. Always measure from permanent monuments
  2. Take multiple measurements of each line
  3. Account for slope when measuring hilly terrain
  4. Document your measurement methods
  5. Verify with independent calculations

Unit Conversion Factors

Professional reference:

  • 1 Beegha = 20 Bisva = 400 Biswansi
  • 1 Acre = 1.6 Beegha (varies by region)
  • 1 Hectare = 2.471 Acres
  • 1 Gattha = 33 sq ft (UP) or 36 sq ft (Bihar)

Always verify local conversion standards.
